04 / 10 How It Works

Three Simple Steps

From deployment to collection — a fully automated cycle that runs 24/7.

Deploy & Float

Released from a base station into oceans, rivers, or coastal zones. Solar-powered, no fuel needed.

Collect Plastic

Conveyor belts pull in debris. AI filters out fish and organic matter. Holds up to 20kg per trip.

Return & Repeat

GPS-guided return to dock. Auto-offload, recharge, and redeploy. Continuous loop, zero human labor.

06 / 10 Features

Built to Survive the Ocean

These aren't lab prototypes. They're engineered for the harshest environment on Earth.

  • Solar-powered hull — unlimited runtime, zero emissions, operates through day and night with battery reserves
  • AI computer vision — real-time object detection that tells plastic from turtles, jellyfish, and seaweed
  • Storm-resistant design — self-righting hull survives waves up to 3 meters, auto-parks in severe weather
  • Modular conveyor system — interchangeable filters for microplastics, bottles, or fishing nets
  • Satellite connectivity — tracked and monitored from anywhere in the world via a central dashboard
  • Recyclable build — drones themselves are made from recycled ocean plastic, closing the loop
